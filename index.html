Hello. I'm Ian Christian Myers and I make fun and interesting things for the web. Recently, I've been doing that over at Zappos.

Zappos Map

Mappos, also known as the Zappos Map, plots orders, in real time, to their destination on a map of the United States. This was a fun side project of mine to explore custom overlays in the Google Maps API, which gained a little attention once the CEO posted on Twitter about it.

Homepage A/B Tests

Hoping to get more out of our beta site's homepage, I created two alternative homepages. These homepages are more product-driven and dynamic than the old homepages, making them much more interesting and inviting.

Checkout

When moving from our "classic" site to our redesigned "zeta" site, one of the more important components was the checkout process. The redesigned checkout process aims to give users access to saved payment and shipping information, to make checkout a more streamlined process.

Faceted Search

The goal of our new faceted search was to move from our existing single-select faceted search and move to a multi-select faceted search that was easier to interact with and made searching for items much faster. The challenge was to take it from concept to production in about a month and build it using the existing architecture.

Zappalytics

At Zappos I've helped organize several Hack-a-Thons in which teams have 36 hours to work on anything Zappos related. Zappalytics is a real time analytics tool that I helped create during one of these Hack-a-Thons. It can display total revenue, amount of orders, 404 errors and 500 errors over the past two days, broken down by every 5 minutes, hour or day.
